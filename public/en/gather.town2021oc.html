In the Interface Design Studio, we conduct basic and applied research on interfaces, which are the interface between users and computers. In modern life, we are constantly required to operate devices, and these devices are not limited to the mouse and keyboard, but also use input devices such as multi-touch, image processing, gesture recognition, and muscle potential manipulation. By developing, implementing, and proposing these various input devices, we nurture students with the design skills to open up the future.


We believe that design is like planting a small seed of "opportunity" for society and the future. At the Interior Design Studio, our starting point is to think about "what it means to live" by connecting humans, society, and nature. We value the sensibility and intelligence of each individual, and through repeated discussions and design trial-and-error. We position design as "the creation of culture through art," and we work on design every day with the aim of supporting people's happiness from both the mental and material aspects. We hope that the students who study design here and leave the nest will become designers who will fascinate the world and sprout wonderful flowers in the future.


In the Ergonomic Design Studio, we consider the relationship between people and objects and systems, and examine what kind of design is necessary for people to feel comfortable. For this purpose, we are studying methods of observing people and understanding their behavior and feelings through an ergonomic approach. Our method is to understand the user's experience, visualize it, and design it. From there, I am working on a design approach to find evidence for the creation of things.


The field of spatial design intersects with existing disciplines such as architecture, landscape architecture, interior design, and public art. Starting with the question, "What is the meaning of spatial design? We approach this multi-disciplinary design field by starting with the question, "What does spatial design mean? Secondly, the cross-disciplinary education students have received in our department is applied to their research activities from a variety of perspectives, so that they can engage in a flexible and interactive design process. This will enable students to better understand their role as designers, establish new design methods, and engage in a variety of design disciplines.


This studio conducts research on the theme of the future of consumer electronics with the goal of integrating products and space. Our goal is to conduct comprehensive research that connects to a wide range of fields such as the plastic arts, science and technology, and the humanities, and to equip students with the ability to solve design problems as well as create products related to these fields. We also aim to find new values for things through meaningful design innovation activities, such as understanding and experiencing the entire process from product planning, design strategy, development, and production.


We conduct research on the design of transportation for the realization of a "beautiful, affluent, and enriched life," using automobiles as his subject material. He teaches practical transportation design by starting with sketches and actually building scale models. As the starting point of my ideas, We aim to combine the sensory part of creating things by hand with the creative part of thinking based on new solutions.We promote education that goes beyond mere re-design by imagining, creating and designing the future of people's lives. In order to realize a truly affluent transportation society, we pursue sustainable design activities from a design perspective, taking into account future urban environments and transportation systems.


With the development and expansion of information communication systems, everyone has become a sender of information, and editing information such as text, images, sound, and video is already an everyday activity in our lives. Based on this premise, we are reconstructing the editing techniques that have been historically developed, along with theoretical analysis, to develop practical methods of knowledge for the information media age. In particular, from the perspective of connecting digital media such as e-books and physical books, and the history of editing technology and culture, we will design/edit a social system for the creation of new value and communication through the effective use of existing sources, and for a new kind of publicity in the age of information sharing.


Interactive Art Studio (a.k.a. IDEEA Lab.) is an acronym for Interactive, Design, Entertainment, Education, Art, and is a laboratory that conducts research that spans the fields of engineering and expression with a focus on interaction design. We are thinking day and night about how interfaces and interactions between humans and computers should be. Specifically, we are developing interfaces that interactively handle touch, image, and sound, and conducting research to create new communication through ideas, design, implementation, verification, and final design output.


The communication environment supported by the Internet has become an indispensable part of our lives. Digital media technology has enriched people's expression and facilitated the transmission of information, but at the same time, people have become increasingly confused by the vast amount of information. At Network Design Studio, we plan and develop contents, applications, and services based on the unique history and culture of digital media expression, while focusing on the discovery of people's creativity and open technological culture.


The relationship between computers and humans is becoming increasingly diverse and deep. In the Software Design Studio, we are studying the relationship between human design activities and computer software. In particular, we are developing a project to support the production of next-generation digital contents. Our research topics include the development of computational algorithms and software that open up new content design, design techniques for efficient development of complex software, and the design of content production processes.


Video images can be broadly categorized into live-action images and animated images depending on the production method. In addition to the commonly seen animation, there are various other production methods such as CG animation and stop motion animation using clay. This course is based on lighting, camera work, editing, planning, world view, character setting, scenario, and storyboarding, and adds knowledge specific to animation to understand the principles of animation and learn the basics of its production methods. In addition to the knowledge of live-action images to be shot for live performances, students will also learn the minimum practical skills of live shooting and editing. In addition, students will understand the structure of planning, world view, character setting, scenario, and storyboarding, which are important in video production, especially in drama production.


In the Visual Communication Design Studio, we conduct research on the theme of how to create new and universal visual communication, based on what kind of ideas and expressions, in the midst of various changes in social structure, cultural awareness, and production systems. We will conduct research on this theme. We will design the possibilities of visual communication that transcends the realm of media, including graphic design, advertising, packaging, products, space and environment. We believe that the wisdom of design is one of the ways to make our daily lives and society more vibrant in today's society.
